## Basic usage

### Setting the text decoration

Control how text is decorated with the `underline`, `no-underline`, and `line-through` utilities.

```html {{ example: true }}
<div class="flex flex-col gap-8">
  <div>
    <span class="font-medium text-sm text-slate-500 font-mono mb-3 dark:text-slate-400">underline</span>
    <p class="underline text-lg font-medium text-slate-900 dark:text-slate-200">
      The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <span class="font-medium text-sm text-slate-500 font-mono mb-3 dark:text-slate-400">overline</span>
    <p class="overline text-lg font-medium text-slate-900 dark:text-slate-200">
      The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <span class="font-medium text-sm text-slate-500 font-mono mb-3 dark:text-slate-400">line-through</span>
    <p class="line-through text-lg font-medium text-slate-900 dark:text-slate-200">
      The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
    </p>
  </div>
  <div>
    <span class="font-medium text-sm text-slate-500 font-mono mb-3 dark:text-slate-400">no-underline</span>
    <p class="text-lg font-medium text-slate-900 dark:text-slate-200">
      The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>
```

```html
<p class="**underline** ...">The quick brown fox ...</p>
<p class="**overline** ...">The quick brown fox ...</p>
<p class="**line-through** ...">The quick brown fox ...</p>
<p class="**no-underline** ...">The quick brown fox ...</p>
```

## <Heading ignore>Accessibility</Heading>

`line-through` produces a visual change that is not compatible with all screen readers. Consider adding additional HTML to make it meaningful for all users.

Suggestion: use [`del`](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTML/Element/del) and [`ins`](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTML/Element/ins), or [`s`](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/HTML/Element/s), following the additional accessibility suggestions provided by MDN.
